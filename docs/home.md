# Home and status

The Home destination is the first customer-facing surface in an Annona tenant. It should orient your team, show whether your tenant is ready, and point to the next best action.

## What Home is for

Use Home to understand:

- whether your tenant is active and ready for work
- which onboarding steps are complete or still needed
- whether recent uploads, datasets, agents, or traces need attention
- where to go next without opening internal admin tools

## First-session checklist

When you first open Annona:

1. Confirm you are in your organization's tenant.
2. Review any onboarding or readiness messages.
3. Upload data if your tenant still needs an initial dataset.
4. Open Datasets to confirm available data.
5. Ask an Agent a focused operational question.
6. Use Traces if you need to understand how a result was produced.

## Customer boundary

Home should summarize customer-facing tenant status only. It should not expose platform deployment details, other customers, admin provisioning, or internal operator runbooks.

## Stable help target

Use `/home/` for in-product help from the tenant home or status surface.
